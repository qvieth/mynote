- When using ChatGPT, you have several options for different types of tasks. Here's a guide on when to use each feature:

## Normal ChatGPT
Use the standard ChatGPT for:
- General conversations and quick questions
- Creative writing tasks
- Coding assistance
- Simple explanations or summaries

## Web Search
Opt for web search when you need:
- Up-to-date information on current events
- Quick facts or simple lookups
- Real-time data like weather or sports scores
- Brief summaries with links to sources

## Reasoning (GPT-4o)
Choose the reasoning model for:
- Complex problem-solving
- Multi-step calculations
- Logical analysis
- Technical tasks requiring advanced capabilities

## Deep Research
Use deep research for:
- Comprehensive reports on complex topics
- Thorough analysis requiring multiple sources
- Niche or non-intuitive information gathering
- Tasks that would typically take hours of human research[2][4]

Deep research is ideal for intensive knowledge work in fields like finance, science, policy, and engineering. It provides fully documented outputs with clear citations, making it perfect for tasks requiring depth, detail, and verification[2][6].

When deciding which option to use, consider the complexity and depth of information you need. For quick answers or casual conversations, stick with normal ChatGPT or web search. For in-depth analysis or something beyond surface level, opt for deep research, which can take 5 to 30 minutes to deliver a thorough, well-documented response[6].

Remember, deep research is currently available to paying ChatGPT users on Plus, Team, Enterprise, and Edu plans, with varying monthly query limits[4][6].
