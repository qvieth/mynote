## 10. Describe a time when you worked successfully as part of a team.
- Situation & Task:
"In 2018, I led a team of four students to compete in the Hult Prize Vietnam. Our goal was to design a social enterprise addressing sustainable energy. Early on, we faced challenges aligning our ideas—everyone had strong opinions, and conflicting solutions caused tension. As a younger leader, I struggled to clarify thoughts and ensure everyone felt heard."

- Action:
Initially, our discussions were unstructured, and misunderstandings slowed progress. For example, I proposed solar-powered, while another argued for biogas solutions, and other propose wind energy. Instead of dismissing ideas, I later introduced a framework: each person had 5 minutes to explain their proposal, followed by open Q&A to clarify assumptions. I also asked teammates to rephrase each other’s points to ensure understanding. While it wasn’t perfect, this reduced friction and helped us merge the best parts of both ideas into a actionable plan

- Result:
"We won 1st place nationally, but the bigger takeaway was learning how communication gaps impact teamwork. Looking back, I’d prioritize active listening earlier and use visual tools (like flowcharts) to map ideas. Now, in projects like my research role at NTU, and marketing executive at NTU debate club I start meetings by setting clear agendas and asking teammates to summarize key points—this ensures alignment and respect for diverse perspectives."

Result:
"The next day, we delivered a cohesive pitch that won the campus round and advanced to nationals. This taught me that pressure often reveals hidden strengths.
